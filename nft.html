Everything you need to know about NFT's

As social media transformed the way we humans communicate in the everyday world. NFTs are changing the way we communicate in the digital space. This latest technology built on blockchain has had immense growth in the last 18 months. Non-fungible tokens (NFTs) have been hitting not only the blockchain headlines recently but also sweeping across mainstream media, as some of the world’s leading celebrities and brands have been accelerating this growing trend. Many successful entrepreneurs like Mark Cuban and Gary Vaynerchuk have been huge promoters of NFTs.

Some might argue the relevance of NFTs in the present-day world. Look at it this way, Fortnite skins add no real value to the gaming experience, yet kids across the world urge their parents to spend hundreds of dollars on them to show off to their friends. Digital ownership is a relatively new concept that is becoming increasingly popular, especially with the younger generations. NFTs take digital ownership to the next level with the help of the blockchain.

What is an NFT?

NFT stands for non-fungible token. it means unique, or non-fungible, digital tokens that run on smart contracts on Ethereum and other blockchains. An nft is proof of ownership of a certain asset. every NFT you own is recorded on blockchain which cannot be altered or duplicated. These assets can be anything like music, sports cards, photos, videos, audio, etc. Like cryptocurrencies, these are stored in digital wallets. and we could buy, sell and trade nft with cryptocurrencies as well.

Who is NFT for?

This is a game-changer for artists, especially digital artists because now they can sell their work directly online to their fans. Even after selling the art, they will be receiving royalties on that NFT for its future trades. Collectors can own a piece of art and have bragging rights just like a normal person who collects paintings. NFTs could also create a new stream for investment and trading.

Within sports, NFTs are taking center stage as NBA Top Shot, an NFT marketplace focusing on the NBA in the US, has raised over $230 million thus far. Notable trades include the NFT for a LeBron James highlight and a Zion Williamson spotlight that sold for $200,000 each. So far, everything from collectible memorabilia to Jack Dorsey’s first tweet has been tokenized even as the market grows. In March, Christie’s Auction house set a record for the highest-selling non-fungible token (NFT) to date, a transaction worth more than $69 million Ethereum tokens. This 1/1 piece dubbed “The First 5000 Days” by Beeple is just one of many NFTs selling at unprecedented amounts. Artists like Beeple are using NFTs to release digital artwork that can be verified authentic through the blockchain.

Where to find NFT?

There are many marketplaces to buy and sell NFTs. Depending on which marketplace you choose, you’ll be able to purchase different types of art or collectibles. A lot of these websites have secondary marketplaces with a variety of NFTs, but each platform operates slightly differently. Here are some of the popular marketplaces.

OpenSea : OpenSea is one of the best and largest NFT marketplaces. It offers a wide range of NFTs based on art, domain names, trading cards, collectibles, and more. It is just the place for you if you want to sell, buy or get your hands on exclusive assets.

How to buy NFTs

Since most NFTs are Ethereum-based tokens, many marketplaces for these collectibles accept Eth tokens as payment. If you already have an account with a cryptocurrency exchange, you can purchase Ethereum on it and send your crypto to your marketplace account.

It’s an easy process to buy an NFT once you’ve funded your account. Most marketplaces are in an auction format, so you’ll need to submit a bid for the NFT you want to purchase. Some marketplaces operate more like an exchange, using the highest bid and lowest ask for NFTs that have several prints.
A benefit of purchasing an NFT from the primary marketplace is the potential resale value directly after the product goes on sale. Some NFTs that are in high demand will sell for 5 to 10 times their initial price right after the release.

The downside to buying NFTs on the primary marketplace is it’s hard to estimate the demand for the art. On the secondary marketplace, you can compare your purchase to previous sales.

Future of NFTs

Non-fungible tokens are much different from other cryptocurrency investments. Many of these tokens don’t have value from their utility like other cryptocurrencies. Instead, NFTs have value because of the media attached to them the most common forms of media on NFTs today are art and music, but NFTs have the potential to tokenize any real-world asset.

Right now most NFTs are used to sell digital art and collectibles. This may be a fad or it could be the new form of exchanging collectible assets ranging from trading cards to artwork.

In the future, NFTs can be used to tokenize any real-world asset, making ownership of assets transparent and incorruptible. Non-fungible tokens could be extremely valuable for real estate deeds, vehicle titles, and business ownership.
